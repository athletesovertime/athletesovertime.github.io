1930's

The sports world changed tremendously due to the Great Depression which started 1929-1939. This caused economic issues while the sport industry struggled to make profit. Even though baseball was popular, many people found it difficult to attend games. forcing a reduction in baseball players' salary. NFL teams suffered financial losses, and several left the league. The Depression did, however, lead to an upsurge in recreational sports involvement since many jobless individuals had more free time. Many taboos relating to female athletics were broken throughout the 1930s. In 1931, the first gymnastics competitions for women took place. The inaugural women's cycling championship was also held in 1939
