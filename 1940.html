1940's




The events of Pearl Harbor, which occurred on December 7, 1941, had an impact on the sports
industry in the 1940s. Even though the majority of professional and collegiate athletes qualified
for the military, they enlisted. Baseball, which had formerly been a well-liked sport, was now in
danger of being discontinued. Professional sports became a significant industry after the war.
Along with the end of the Great Depression, sporting event attendance was once again on the rise.
American sports gradually merged with the entertainment industry. Overall there were major
advancements, Such as new aircraft technology and the widespread use of television, causing
top players' incomes to skyrocket.
